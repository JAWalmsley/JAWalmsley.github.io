Some Common Computer Ports

External Ports
VGA Used for older monitors
DVI A newer standard than VGA, but still older than HDMI
HDMI One of the current standards for displays, supports a higher resolution and refresh rate than the older standards. Requires royalties to use.
PS/2 Older keyboad and mouse ports, now usually replaced by USB though they technically are faster

Keyboard in purple, mouse in green
USB 3 Newer, faster version of USB2
USB 2 Older, slower version of USB3
Serial Very old type of port, most commonly used for printers or modems. Basically old USB.

Not to be confused with VGA, which looks similar
DisplayPort Similar to HDMI, but is an open standard which means no royalties.
Thunderbolt Uses the same connectors as Mini DisplayPort or USB Type-C, but serves as a display and power cable all in one.
Sound(7.1) Surround sound output, with ports for 7 directional speakers, plus subwoofer
Sound(Mic, line in, and Headphone) Sound input and output.
Internal Ports
SATA Used for data to and from internal storage such as hard drives or disk drives
IDE Data transfer to and from internal storage. Parallel instead of serial like SATA is, so it's much slower.
Motherboard Power Delivers power from the power supply to the motherboard so it can power all the various things plugged into it.
PCI Express Slots A large slot with high bandwidth for expansion cards. X16 is the highest bandwidth, commonly for graphics cards; X8 is for smaller cards that don't require as much throughput, such as network cards.

X16 on top, X8 in the middle. The one on the bottom is X1, for sound cards and such
Hard Drive Power Supplies power to hard drives, solid state drives, disk drives and if you're cool, RGB case lights.
